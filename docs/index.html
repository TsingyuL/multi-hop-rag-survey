Central claim

Architecture labels are not enough to explain multi-hop RAG.

A system can retrieve relevant documents yet fail because a supporting fact was dropped, hidden by context, fused incorrectly, or never causally used. The survey makes these failures separately visible.

01 / A COMMON OBJECT

Latent evidence chain

We treat the chain of required support units as the object a system must recover and use—not merely a sequence of model modules.

02 / A DIAGNOSTIC LENS

Five coupled bottlenecks

Observability, selection, exposure, fusion, and faithfulness turn aggregate scores into specific, testable failure modes.

03 / AN AUDITABLE MAP

Methods, benchmarks, decisions

Each reviewed record is linked to the decision it changes, its evidence source, task setting, and available implementation.

A diagnostic vocabulary

Ask where the evidence chain breaks.

Use the taxonomy to separate retrieval coverage from selection quality, context accessibility, reasoning, and causal use. These estimands are coupled, but they should not be conflated.
